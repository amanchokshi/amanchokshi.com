About Me

I am a PhD student at The University of Melbourne, with Prof. Rachel Webster. My research interests involve radio astronomy, observational cosmology and instrumentation. My current project aims to calibrate the directional and polarised response of the Murchison Widefield Array (MWA) antennas, using communication satellites. Precise and prior understanding of instrumental effects are critical to the analysis of large data volumes, aimed at detecting subliminal cosmic signals from the Epoch of Reionization (EoR). My PhD research will explore these instrumental effects via simulations and develop analyses pipelines to lower the current EoR limit and improve the polarised response of the telescopes such as the MWA. These studies can potentially inform the development of the proposed Square Kilometre Array.
